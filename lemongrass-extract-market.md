<p><p>The lemongrass extract market comprises two primary types: oil and hydrosol. Lemongrass oil is derived through steam distillation, capturing the plant's potent aromatic properties, making it popular in aromatherapy, cosmetics, and culinary applications. Hydrosol, or floral water, is the byproduct of the distillation process, containing diluted essential oils and water-soluble compounds, offering milder fragrances and therapeutic benefits. Both forms serve diverse industries, including food and beverage, personal care, and wellness, catering to growing consumer demand for natural products.</p></p>
