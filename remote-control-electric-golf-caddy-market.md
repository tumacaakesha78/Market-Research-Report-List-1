<p><p>The remote-control electric golf caddy market primarily features two battery types: Sealed Lead Acid (SLA) and Lithium-Ion (Li-ion). SLA batteries are traditional, offering durability and lower initial costs, but are heavier and less efficient. In contrast, Li-ion batteries are lighter, have a longer lifespan, and provide better energy efficiency, making them increasingly popular among modern caddy users. Both types cater to different consumer preferences, impacting performance, weight, and overall user experience in golf courses.</p></p>
